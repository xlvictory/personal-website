I had taken an art class my sophomore year of college (2017) as a creative outlet, since my STEM classes were very intense and rigorous. It was nice to have a little break in all the craziness. Plus, I LOVE to paint, so naturally this was a the perfect class. This assignment, we had to replicate a painting from a surrealism artist and add our own twist. I had chosen the painting “The Butterfly Apple” by Salvador Dali. He’s one of my favorite painters. My personal twist on my project was my material. I had no black paint, and all the black paint I tried to create DEFINITELY was not black, but rather a very murky and muddy brown, no matter what I tried. Instead, I used my liquid eyeliner and a paint mixing medium and painted with that. My professor and peers were so impressed by my quick thinking and dedication to the project. It’s personally one of my favorite projects to date.
