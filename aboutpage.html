More Info

Born and raised in the Bible Belt of the United States for majority of my life, I grew up with a set of values that some would call traditional. Hospitality, compassion, respect and etiquette was something that was instilled in me at a young age. Especially because I was born and only child to a single mother who worked constantly to make a living for us; she ensured that I was a refined young lady. Not saying that I’m not, but I think I was a bit too eccentric in my mannerisms, that my mother and I moved to California with my grandparents. I knew I was different and I was absolutely okay with that.

I’m the type to have my hands in multiple pots of life; variety is one of the major things in life that I need to stay focused. My music taste is large and ranges across genres (although my favorite is metal and k-pop). I also have hobbies and interests that span as well. I am interested in everything from the arts and entertainment, to STEM, to the occult, to sports (I was in gymnastics for 10 years) and even food and travel. Being well-rounded is one of the biggest things about me and my personality.

I went to college in Houston, TX and double majored in Biomedical Engineering and Mathematics with a minor in astrophysics (just because I thought it’d be interesting). I have a couple years left, but I took a hiatus from university because my mom had stage 4 cancer metatstatic breast cancer. I rushed home to take care of her, because I didn’t know just how much time I had left. I spent years with her as she beat cancer 2 times after that— she struggled with cancer 4 times total. She’s in remission now and begged me to live my own life, so I wanted to go back to school. I enrolled in the Grace Hopper Program at Fullstack Academy to kickstart my education again. I’m a self-taught programmer (self-taught like most of my skills), and wanted to enhance the information I already know to build a career in tech that will not only help me take care of my small little family, but also help me prepare for my future, by providing me with the income to pay for my schooling and the knowledge to complete the courses needed to follow my dreams.

Overall, I think I’m pretty okay!! I have lived quite a life so far, at my big age of 25, and I thank the Universe for providing me with the opportunities to better myself. Things in life do get better (even incrementally), and I am looking forward to the development it has for me. Thanks for listening :-).
